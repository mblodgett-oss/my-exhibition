## Henry Sugimoto's Interpretation of Japanese American Internment

During World War II, more than 120,000 Japanese Americans were forcibly removed from their homes and incarcerated in camps during the Japanese American Internment. Even as their loyalty to the United States was questioned, many still felt pressure to prove their patriotism through military service, sacrifice, and cultural assimilation. Through the paintings of Henry Sugimoto, this site examines the contradictions faced by Japanese Americans who were asked to demonstrate loyalty to a nation that had already denied them full acceptance, revealing the emotional and moral tensions of identity, belonging, and citizenship during wartime.
